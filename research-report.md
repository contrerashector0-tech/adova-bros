## 1. Who They Are — Story, Food & Services

### Business Overview
**Adova Bros.** is a locally owned and operated food truck business based in El Paso, TX, registered as an LLC. Their tagline is **"Quality. Quantity. Satisfaction."** with a secondary line: *"Simple, affordable, memorable"* — suggesting a value-forward, community-oriented brand.

The business offers **three core services:**
- **Food Truck** — mobile vending at community events, markets, bars, apartment complexes
- **Catering** — private and corporate event catering
- **Dry Bartending** — non-alcoholic beverage service for events (mocktails, specialty drinks, juice bars, etc.)

### Known Event History
| Venue | Type |
|-------|------|
| Coronado Villa Townhomes | Apartment community event |
| Bronco Swap Meet (8408 Alameda Ave) | Major El Paso flea market, 300+ vendors, weekends 7am–6pm |
| Local YMCA events | Community/family events (dry bartending likely featured here) |
| Rockabilly Bop Market — El Paso Coliseum | Niche cultural market (vintage cars, live music, pin-up) |
| Mangos Dive Bar (1510 Bengal Dr) | Bar pop-up |
| Marvel Universe Market — Oasis Lanes | Niche pop culture market |

This lineup shows Adova Bros actively targeting **community events, apartment complexes, niche markets, and neighborhood pop-ups** — a grassroots street-level strategy rather than a fixed-location model.

### Food & Menu
The actual menu items are not publicly documented, but the following is known:
- They announced a **new menu item** in a recent Instagram post (caption: *"adding a new menu item this year! Introducing..."*)
- Their Square site includes a dedicated **Menu page** and **Gallery page** — content was not accessible via scraping
- The combination of food truck + catering + dry bartending suggests a **flexible, full-event menu** rather than a single specialty item focus

**Intelligence gap:** The specific cuisine type (Mexican street food, burgers, fusion, etc.) is not yet confirmed from public sources. This should be clarified directly with the client.

### Dry Bartending — A Key Differentiator
Dry bartending (non-alcoholic bar service) is relatively uncommon as a bundled food truck add-on in El Paso. Competitors in this space (My Mobile Bartender, Bar & Tender Mobile Co., With A Twist Bartending Service) operate independently — **no other El Paso food truck appears to bundle food + dry bar as a combined package.** This is a genuine competitive advantage worth highlighting on the new website.

Dry bartending is especially relevant for:
- YMCA and school events (alcohol-free zones)
- Quinceañeras and baptisms (family events with minors)
- Corporate/office events
- Sober celebrations and dry weddings
- Military family events (Fort Bliss community)

## 3. Competitor Analysis — El Paso Food Trucks

### Market Overview
- **40–50+ food trucks** operating in El Paso
- Highly fragmented — no dominant brand
- Mexican/taco trucks are the largest segment by far
- Growing demand for novelty cuisines (Filipino, Jamaican, Mediterranean, fusion)
- Average catering booking in El Paso: **~$1,100** (range: $800–$2,500)

## 4. Website & SEO Recommendations

### Why a New Website is Essential
The current Square site has significant problems:
1. **Business category listed as "clothing-shoes"** — actively hurts local SEO
2. **Subdomain only** (adova-bros.square.site) — no custom domain authority
3. **No updated content** since events through May 2025
4. **No structured menu data** — Google can't index the menu
5. **No Google Business Profile integration** evident
6. **No customer reviews** or social proof visible

### Recommended Website Structure
```
Homepage           → Hero + tagline + CTA ("Book Us / See Our Menu")
Menu               → Full menu with descriptions, photos, prices
Services           → Food Truck / Catering / Dry Bartending (separate sections)
Catering Packages  → Pricing tiers, minimum headcounts, what's included
Gallery            → Photos of food, events, setup
Events / Schedule  → Where to find them this week/month
About Us           → The brothers' story, why they started, their values
Contact / Book     → Inquiry form + phone + email + social links
```

### Custom Domain Recommendation
Register: **adovabros.com** or **adovabros.food** or **adovabroscatering.com**

### Google Business Profile (Critical — Must Do First)
Before launching a new website, Adova Bros must:
1. Claim/create their **Google Business Profile** at business.google.com
2. Set category to: **Food Truck** (primary) + **Catering Food and Drink Supplier**
3. Add service area: El Paso, TX
4. Upload 10–15 photos of food, truck, and events
5. Add full menu with prices
6. Collect first 10+ Google reviews from past customers
7. Link to new website once live

This alone will drive significant discovery — Google Maps is the #1 way locals find food vendors.

### Social Media Recommendations
- **Instagram:** Post 3–4x per week. Prioritize: menu photos, short Reels of food prep, event countdowns, "find us this week" schedule posts
- **Facebook:** Create recurring Facebook Events for every scheduled appearance — free discoverability
- **TikTok:** Create account now — food prep videos, "day in the life of a food truck" content performs extremely well and has near-zero competition in El Paso food truck space
- **Google Reviews:** Actively ask happy customers to leave Google reviews at every event (QR code on truck pointing to review page)

### Website Tech Recommendation
For a small food truck operation, recommend:
- **Squarespace** or **Webflow** (custom domain, clean templates, easy menu management)
- OR a hand-built static site (as this project appears to be — `index.html` + `style.css` + `main.js`) with hosting on **Netlify** or **Vercel** (free, fast, custom domain)
- Add **Schema.org structured data** for `LocalBusiness`, `FoodEstablishment`, and `Menu` — this feeds Google rich results directly
